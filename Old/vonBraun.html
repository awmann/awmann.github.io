A Cool Stars 18 Splinter Session
Kaspar von Braun
Radii of Planet Hosts from Interferometry
Just as precious metals, when brought into contact with touchstones, leave traces on their surfaces, planets, when in orbit around stars, leave observable signatures. It is almost exclusively through the study of these signatures that extrasolar planets can be characterized. The knowledge of the astrophysical parameters of the host stars is therefore crucial to any insights into the planetary system: “at best, you only understand the planets as well as you understand the parent stars.” Interferometric studies of extrasolar planet hosts, coupled with trigonometric parallaxes and multi-band literature photometry, provide directly determined values of stellar diameters and effective temperatures. This talk illustrates the methods and approaches of our ongoing survey of planet hosts with the CHARA Array. Particularly interesting targets are (1) the parent stars of transiting planets since a full characterization of stellar and planetary astrophysical parameters is made possible by the transit aspect, and (2) late-type parent stars since their astrophysical parameters (and thus the parameters of the planetary system) are less predictable by current stellar models.
