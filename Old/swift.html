The Kepler Space Mission has provided and extraordinary dataset with scientific value that extends well beyond the field of exoplanets. In addition to more than 100 planets orbiting low mass stars, Kepler has also discovered tens of low-mass stellar eclipsing systems spanning a wide period range that provide an accurate, model-independent means of measuring the fundamental properties of low-mass stars. For this study, we concentrate on the detached systems with periods > 2 days that fill in a large gap in parameter space for low-mass eclipsing binaries. We will present results from Kepler photometry supplemented with data from a suite of ongoing observational followup programs that facilitate a direct comparison between measured masses, radii, effective temperatures and metallicities to stellar evolution models in the context of several other measured properties including rotational periods and activity levels. Lastly, we will take a look ahead to the near future as we approach the peak observing season for the Kepler field.
